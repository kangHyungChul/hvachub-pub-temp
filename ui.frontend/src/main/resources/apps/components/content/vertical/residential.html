Detached House Solution

[Floor Plan]

Single-Family Homes (Detached Houses) are standalone residential ​structures designed for individual families, offering privacy, ​independence, and personalized living spaces. These homes typically​ feature separate entrances, private yards, and dedicated areas for ​living, sleeping, and recreational activities.

When designing HVAC systems for single-family homes, key factors ​to consider include:

  • Smart home integration and user convenience Tailor the system to the home’s layout, ensuring even air distribution ​ and zoning for different areas.
  • Energy Efficiency Use technologies like variable speed drives and smart thermostats. ​
  • Comfort and Air Quality Maintain optimal humidity levels and improve indoor air quality with ​air purification systems.

Semi-detached & Town House Solution​

[Floor Plan]
  • ① Semi-Detached House​

    A semi-detached house is a type of house where two houses share one side, with each house sharing a wall with another. Common in urban areas, they are seen as an efficient way to maximize housing in limited spaces.

  • ② Town House

    A townhouse is a type of row house that extends horizontally along a street, with each floor having its own living space and family members sharing the vertical space. They typically offer more square footage than apartments and provide privacy.


    Important considerations to take into account when designing a house HVAC system include:

  • Smart home integration and user convenience
  • Efficient heating and cooling, hot water supply that aligns with local regulations
  • Cost performance
  • Indoor air quality
  • Comfort and low noise
  • Both individual and centralized control

Flat House & Condominium Solution​

[Floor Plan]
  • ① Flat house​​

    A flat house is a multi-story building with multiple independent residences, each individually owned and having its own entrance. Popular in urban areas for their efficient use of space and proximity to amenities, they offer a more affordable housing option compared to stand-alone single-family homes, making them attractive for city living.

  • ② Condominium ​

    A type of housing where individual units are owned privately, while common areas are jointly owned by all unit owners. This arrangement allows for personal ownership within the property while sharing responsibilities and costs with other residents.​​


    Important considerations to take into account when designing a house HVAC system include:

  • Smart home integration and user convenience​
  • Efficient heating and cooling, hot water supply that aligns with​ local regulations​
  • Cost performance​
  • Comfort and low noise, indoor air quality
  • Both individual and centralized control
